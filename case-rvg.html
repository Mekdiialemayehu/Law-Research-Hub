R v G and Another (2003)

Facts

Two boys aged 11 and 12 lit newspapers while camping. The fire spread and caused damage.

Legal Issue

The key issue was whether recklessness should be judged subjectively (what the defendant actually foresaw) or objectively (what a reasonable person would have foreseen).

Decision

The House of Lords held that recklessness should be judged subjectively, changing the previous legal approach.

Significance

This case modernised the law of mens rea in criminal law and replaced the objective test with a subjective one for recklessness.
